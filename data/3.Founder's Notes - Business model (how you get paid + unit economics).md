## Part 3) Business model (how you get paid + unit economics)

**Snippet:** This is the translation layer between value and cash. A product that customers love can still fail if you don’t capture value, if cash timing kills you, or if you scale losses.  
**Question:** *For each incremental sale, do you make money, and do you get paid in a way that can scale?*

---

## A high-level execution system (principles + action items)

### 1) Start with value capture (not vibes)
**Principle:** Creating value isn’t enough—you must capture some of it ([Conspiracy + Zero to One](https://www.foundersnotes.com/transcripts/2256/?location=02:00:18)).  
**Action items:**
- Write a 1-page “money map”: **who pays, for what, when, how often, and why now**.
- Choose your primary pricing lever: **usage, subscription, take-rate, per-seat, margin-on-goods, service fee**. Keep it simple.

### 2) Avoid commodity competition
**Principle:** Competitive markets destroy profits ([Zero to One](https://www.foundersnotes.com/bookreview/1570/?highlight=58448)). Great businesses are built around a “secret” outsiders don’t see ([Zero to One](https://www.foundersnotes.com/bookreview/1570/?highlight=54558)).  
**Action items:**
- Define your “secret”: the specific reason you can win (distribution edge, cost edge, unique supply, brand, speed, integration).
- If your pitch is “same as X but cheaper,” you’re probably in a profit-destroying lane unless you have a structural cost advantage.

### 3) Measure unit economics early (and obsess over the few that matter)
**Principle:** Results matter; stay laser-focused on what matters ([How to Make a Few Billion Dollars](https://www.foundersnotes.com/bookreview/1351/?highlight=43471)).  
**Action items (keep it tight):**
- Track 5 numbers weekly:
  1) **Gross margin** (after direct costs)  
  2) **CAC** (customer acquisition cost)  
  3) **Payback period** (CAC / contribution margin per period)  
  4) **Retention** (or repeat rate)  
  5) **Cash conversion cycle** (how long cash is tied up)
- Make CAC an experiment-driven discipline: “run experiments” to drive it down ([Scientific Advertising / Claude Hopkins](https://www.foundersnotes.com/transcripts/2219/?location=00:08:04)).

### 4) Iterate fast toward the winning model
**Principle:** The faster you experiment and kill what doesn’t work, the faster you reach the winning model ([In the Company of Giants](https://www.foundersnotes.com/bookreview/1448/?highlight=49509)). Don’t let planning block doing ([Bloomberg by Bloomberg](https://www.foundersnotes.com/bookreview/1428/?highlight=48362)); treat entrepreneurship as experimentation ([Zero to One](https://www.foundersnotes.com/bookreview/1570/?highlight=58486)).  
**Action items:**
- Run in 2-week loops: **hypothesis → test → measure → keep/kill**.
- Optimize to “fail well” (learn without getting knocked out) ([Ray Dalio: Principles](https://www.foundersnotes.com/transcripts/2034/?location=00:08:08)).

### 5) Treat distribution as first-class (equal to product)
**Principle:** “Sales matters just as much as product” ([Zero to One](https://www.foundersnotes.com/bookreview/1570/?highlight=58448)). One distribution strategy often dominates others ([Peter Thiel episode](https://www.foundersnotes.com/transcripts/2237/?location=00:41:58)).  
**Action items:**
- Pick *one* primary channel to win first (outbound, inbound, partnerships, retail, marketplace, virality).
- Write your “funnel math” on one page: **lead → conversion → revenue → contribution**.

### 6) Build the model to survive cash stress (cash timing is part of the model)
**Principle:** Sloppy financial control can sink fast-growing businesses ([The Billionaire Who Wasn’t](https://www.foundersnotes.com/bookreview/1552/?highlight=53725)). Forced growth can be fatal ([How To Be Rich](https://www.foundersnotes.com/bookreview/2334/?highlight=86745)).  
**Action items:**
- Put basic controls in place early: **bookkeeping, per-unit profitability, cash runway**, and “where the cash goes.”
- Don’t scale what you haven’t proven makes money.

### 7) Make reinvestment and cash deployment explicit
**Principle:** Great CEOs do two things: run operations efficiently and deploy the cash generated ([The Outsiders](https://www.foundersnotes.com/bookreview/1618/?highlight=51064)). Getty: reinvest, be personally involved, practice thrift ([J. Paul Getty: The Richest Private Citizen in America](https://www.foundersnotes.com/transcripts/2348/?location=00:30:02)).  
**Action items:**
- Decide your default: **reinvest in what works** until returns fall.
- Use “economy” as a strategy, not a mood ([How To Be Rich](https://www.foundersnotes.com/bookreview/2334/?highlight=86745)).

### 8) Write down the principles you’ll use to make tradeoffs
**Principle:** Written principles keep a complicated enterprise on course ([Ogilvy on Advertising](https://www.foundersnotes.com/bookreview/1614/?highlight=57601)); being principled means operating with explainable principles ([Ray Dalio: Principles](https://www.foundersnotes.com/transcripts/2034/?location=00:00:01)).  
**Action items:**
- Write 5–10 “business model principles” (e.g., *“CAC must pay back in X months,” “we lower price only when efficiency improves,” “no forced growth”*).
- Use them to decide quickly under pressure.
