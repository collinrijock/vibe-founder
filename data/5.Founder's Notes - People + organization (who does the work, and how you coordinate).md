*Initial snippet:* “It’s people who build an organization; an organization builds a business” ([Time to Make the Donuts](https://www.foundersnotes.com/bookreview/1425/?highlight=48079))  
*Question to keep asking:* **Do you have the right people in the right “shape” of org to move fast without losing control?**

## A high-level execution system for Part 5 (People + Organization)

### 1) Set the founder standard (your behavior is the baseline)
- **Commit fully**; your intensity becomes the organization’s ceiling ([Sam Walton: Made In America](https://www.foundersnotes.com/transcripts/2287/?location=01:47:58)).  
- **Be uncompromised about the work that matters** (high commitment or it disappears) ([Bill Gates](https://www.foundersnotes.com/transcripts/2056/?location=00:34:02)).  
- **Make sure the mission is real enough to sustain the grind**—passion isn’t optional ([Steve Jobs, Bill Gates, Michael Dell, Bill Gates, Andy Grove, Bill Hewlett](https://www.foundersnotes.com/transcripts/2071/?location=00:30:07)).

Action item: write (1) what “excellent” looks like here, (2) what you will not tolerate, and live it in public.

---

### 2) Hire (and keep) talent density; don’t negotiate with mediocrity
- The problem isn’t “bad people,” it’s **too many 240 hitters**—raise the bar ([Warren Buffett and Charlie Munger](https://www.foundersnotes.com/transcripts/2299/?location=00:20:08)).  
- **Hire up, not down** (avoid “a company of dwarfs”) ([A Few Lessons From Warren Buffett](https://www.foundersnotes.com/transcripts/2301/?location=00:42:08)).  
- Build a pipeline by **talking to rival talent** (it’s intelligence gathering *and* recruiting) ([Felix Dennis (How to Get Rich)](https://www.foundersnotes.com/transcripts/1994/?location=00:49:59)).

Action item: define 3 non-negotiable traits for every role; if a candidate misses any, pass.

---

### 3) Align incentives so people act like owners
- Treat key people like **partners** and share upside ([Sam Walton: Made In America](https://www.foundersnotes.com/transcripts/2287/?location=01:47:58)).  
- Owners collaborate on **economics**; non-owners fight for **control**—design accordingly ([John Malone (Cable Cowboy)](https://www.foundersnotes.com/transcripts/2170/?location=01:05:24)).  
- Don’t confuse recognition with bribery; praise what’s truly excellent ([Felix Dennis (How to Get Rich)](https://www.foundersnotes.com/transcripts/1994/?location=00:49:59)).

Action item: for every team, explicitly answer: “How do they win if the company wins?”

---

### 4) Pick an org “shape” that matches speed: small teams + decentralized command
- Use human-scale units: ~**12** for tight execution; ~**150** for cohesion ([Yvon Chouinard: What We've Learned from Patagonia's First 40 Years](https://www.foundersnotes.com/transcripts/2157/?location=00:52:00)).  
- Push decisions down by explaining **why** (people act better when they understand why) ([Charlie Munger (the NEW Poor Charlie's Almanack)](https://www.foundersnotes.com/transcripts/2028/?location=00:42:08)).

Action item: redesign work into “teams that can ship”—each with a single accountable owner and clear decision rights.

---

### 5) Create truth flow (bad news must travel faster than good news)
- Demand truth; avoid yes-men ([I Had Dinner With Michael Ovitz](https://www.foundersnotes.com/transcripts/2753/?location=00:20:02)).  
- Build a habit of **welcoming bad news**; “say the negatives” ([Mr. Beast Leaked Memo](https://www.foundersnotes.com/transcripts/2362/?location=00:23:59)).  
- Standardize clarity: **who/what/where/when/why**—and don’t omit “why” ([Charlie Munger (the NEW Poor Charlie's Almanack)](https://www.foundersnotes.com/transcripts/2028/?location=00:42:08)).

Action item: install one “unfiltered channel” (direct email/voicemail/office hours) and publicly reward the first person to surface a real problem.

---

### 6) Run execution with simple accountability loops
- Convert input into action: list initiatives as **workstreams with named owners** ([How to Make a Few More Billion Dollars: Brad Jacobs](https://www.foundersnotes.com/transcripts/3314/?location=segment_5)).  
- Keep feedback lightweight: quarterly (or post-change) survey with 3 questions—what works, what’s broken, best idea ([How to Make a Few More Billion Dollars: Brad Jacobs](https://www.foundersnotes.com/transcripts/3314/?location=segment_5)).  
- Practice “wrap it up”: decide, close, move on ([Mind Your Own Business](https://www.foundersnotes.com/bookreview/1427/?highlight=48289)).

Action item: weekly review = (1) top priorities, (2) blocked items, (3) decisions needed, (4) owners + dates.

---

### 7) Protect the org from bloat (overhead eats culture)
- “Overhead walks on 2 legs”—prune it or it grows by osmosis ([Felix Dennis (How to Get Rich)](https://www.foundersnotes.com/transcripts/1994/?location=00:49:59)).  
- Daily compounding is real: eliminate unnecessary costs (and keep improving) ([The Essays of Warren Buffett](https://www.foundersnotes.com/transcripts/2196/?location=00:30:16)).

Action item: once a quarter, kill/merge one recurring meeting/process/team activity that no longer pays for itself.
